My Learning Plan & Goals


My goal is to break into the workforce in IT. I really enjoy the creative aspects
and freedom that a job in web design would entail. I can easily visualise myself carving out a
career with the skills Dev Academy offers to their students. A definite learning strength is my
ability to pick up concepts quickly and run with them. Learning new ideas tends to be on the
simpler end of the spectrum and I learn especially well when I see the value in the information presented.

The other side of the coin is that I struggle to learn new things when motivation lacks. When my
motivation is lacking, let's just say I get really good sleep-ins!
I’m easily distracted which is why I incorporate the 25/5 minute pomodoros into my learning habits. This helps
retain focus, avoid distractions and also be more productive in that 5 minutes of time I earn for myself.
I would like to develop communication skills. This is a progressive, long-term personal goal
that would see my confidence grow like LeBron. The goal here is not extroversion, but to be
less introverted, and be a hooman that doesn’t struggle and overthink in simple conversations
I love the concept of respect and this foundational principle is why I will commit myself
to this program. I am aware of the group project work in the boot camp period of the course
and the work the all the course facilitators put in behind the scenes. Respect is putting in the
effort for my team when the time comes. Respect is putting in the hard yards now bridging the
knowledge gap (the gap being where I am now, and where I need to be in 3 weeks), and respect is
demonstrating to the facilitators that they did not make a mistake giving me a spot in this program
over another aspiring person. Respect will also be my main source of navigation in conversations
with peers, lectures and facilitators. Work-wise, pomodoros will take me far into this program, as they
have proven to be an ace for me in the past. I have a strong network of support at home and the facilitators
at Dev Academy have already demonstrated their desire to converse and help us. When things get hard,
I like to eat food (nothing quite calms the mind like a good hot pizza) and talk through it with my peeps.
I’m not afraid to ask for help, especially in cases where I can confidently say “I have tried” or
“I’ve been at it for hours”.

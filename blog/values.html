Short Piece on Values


For myself, authenticity is a core value. I have been working on delivering a more
natural, real version of myself to new people I meet, to people I consider my friends, and
to myself and those dearest to me. I grew up shy, self-conscious and fearful of opinions about me for
the sports I played, or games I loved and the decisions I think are correct. This is
a work in progress as I continue to develop key communication skills and become more and more
confident in my own opinions and build a general feeling of appreciation for who I am. I enjoy people who are open
about themselves and their interests, fears and history, as these conversations serve as learning curves and a
gateway into who I could be and how I could present myself to others.
I joined Dev Academy as a student eager to gain new skills not only in the field of technology, but
also in areas such as team building and group project work. I am currently doing another course which is
fantastic in terms of the rounded approach to IT it gives, but it is easy to feel isolated in a relatively
closed setting. Most feedback I have received about Dev Academy speaks to the contrary which greatly
interests, excites and motivates me to be here part of the Roa cohort adventure.
